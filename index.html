Why Does It Matter?

In a democracy, you would expect to voters choose their representatives. In reality, the representatives are the the ones who choose their voters.

In our current system, the parties in power can draw maps as they see fit to give them huge advantages over opponents. This means that those who gain power stay in power. Out of 435 House seats, only a mere 33-40 are seriously in play. In fact, there are more uncontested seats than competitive ones, and most districts see little or no advertizing whatsoever.
